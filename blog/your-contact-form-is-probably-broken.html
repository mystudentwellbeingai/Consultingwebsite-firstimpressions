Is Your Contact Form Broken? When Was the Last Time You Checked?

Here's a question: when was the last time you actually tested your website's contact form? Not glanced at it. Actually filled it out, hit submit, and confirmed the message landed in your inbox.

If you can't remember, you're not alone. Most business owners set up their contact form once and never touch it again. And that's a problem, because forms break silently.

How forms break without warning

There are a dozen ways a contact form can stop working:

  • A plugin or website update changes a setting
  • Your email provider starts flagging form submissions as spam
  • A security certificate expires and breaks the form's connection
  • Your web host updates something on the server side
  • The form provider itself has an outage or changes their API

None of these send you a notification. The form still looks fine on your website. Visitors fill it out, hit submit, maybe even see a "thank you" message. But the submission never reaches you.

The real cost

Think about what a single new client is worth to your business. For a therapist, one new client could mean thousands of dollars over the course of treatment. For a small business, a single lead could be the difference between a good month and a slow one.

Now imagine your form has been broken for a week. Or two. How many people tried to reach you, got no response, and moved on to someone else? You'll never know, because from your end, it just looked like a quiet period.

The fix is simple

Test your form regularly. Submit it yourself. Confirm the message arrives. Check that your auto-reply goes out if you have one.
