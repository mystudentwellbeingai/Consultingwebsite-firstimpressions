See the Difference

What your clients actually read.

Most provider websites lead with credentials and clinical language. Here's what that looks like to someone searching for help at 11pm on a Tuesday.

Before

"I'm a Registered Social Worker with a strength-based approach, experienced in Cognitive Behavioural Therapy and Solution-Focused Brief Therapy. I hold a Master of Social Work and am a member of the Ontario College of Social Workers and Social Service Workers."

What a potential client is thinking:

  • "What's the difference between a Social Worker and a Psychotherapist? Should I care?"
  • "What is a strength-based approach? Is that even relevant to my situation?"
  • "I've heard of CBT but I have no idea what Solution-Focused Brief Therapy is."
  • "This feels like a resume, not someone who understands what I'm going through."
After

"Feeling stuck, overwhelmed, or not sure where to start? I help people work through anxiety, life transitions, and the kind of stress that builds up quietly. Together, we'll focus on what's already working in your life and build from there. At your pace, on your terms."

Why this works:

  • Leads with the client's experience, not the therapist's credentials
  • Uses plain language anyone can relate to
  • "Strength-based approach" becomes "focus on what's already working." Same method, human words
  • Feels like the first step toward help, not a clinical intake form
Before

"My practice utilizes evidence-based modalities including EMDR, DBT, and ACT to treat generalized anxiety disorder, major depressive disorder, and PTSD in adult populations."

What a potential client is thinking:

  • "I don't know what any of those acronyms mean."
  • "Do I have a 'disorder'? That feels heavy. Maybe this isn't for me."
  • "'Adult populations'... am I a population?"
  • "This sounds like it was written for an insurance company, not for me."
After

"If you're dealing with anxiety that won't quiet down, sadness that's hard to shake, or memories that still affect your day-to-day, I can help. I use a few different approaches depending on what fits you best, and I'll explain everything along the way. No jargon, no pressure."

Why this works:

  • Describes symptoms the way people actually experience them
  • Avoids diagnostic labels that can feel intimidating to someone seeking help for the first time
  • "Evidence-based modalities" becomes "a few different approaches." Accessible and reassuring
  • The promise of "no jargon, no pressure" directly addresses the wall most provider sites put up

These are the kinds of issues I catch in every audit. Let's look at your site.

FAQ

Common questions.

How long does an audit take?

Most homepage audits are delivered within 3 business days. Full site audits depend on the number of pages, but I'll give you a timeline upfront before we start.

What format is the report?

You'll receive a written PDF report with specific observations, screenshots where relevant, and prioritized recommendations. It's designed to be actionable, not overwhelming.

Do you fix the issues you find, or just report them?

I focus on identifying issues and giving you clear recommendations. I don't do development or design work, but my reports are detailed enough to hand directly to your web developer or designer.

What happens if my contact form fails during monitoring?

I'll notify you immediately by email so you can address it right away. Every day a broken form goes unnoticed is a day potential clients can't reach you.

I'm not a therapist. Can you still help?

Absolutely. While I specialize in mental health provider websites, the fundamentals of first impressions, clear messaging, and working contact forms apply to any business. I work with small businesses across industries.

Can I bundle an audit with monitoring?

Yes. If you'd like both services, reach out and we can put together something that works for you.
